What is an LLC Operating Agreement?

An LLC operating agreement is a legal document that outlines the ownership structure, management procedures, and operational rules for your limited liability company. While not always required by state law, it's essential for protecting your business and defining how your LLC will operate.

Liability Protection

Helps maintain the "corporate veil" that protects your personal assets from business liabilities.

Ownership Structure

Clearly defines member ownership percentages, voting rights, and management responsibilities.

Dispute Resolution

Establishes procedures for handling conflicts and making important business decisions.

Business Growth

Provides a framework for adding new members, selling ownership, and expanding operations.

Why You Need One
  • Protects personal assets
  • Prevents member disputes
  • Defines profit distribution
  • Required by many banks
  • Overrides state defaults
  • Professional credibility

Essential Sections of an Operating Agreement

Every comprehensive operating agreement should include these key sections.

1
Basic Information
  • LLC name and address
  • Purpose and business activities
  • State of formation
  • Effective date
  • Registered agent information
2
Member Information
  • Member names and addresses
  • Ownership percentages
  • Initial capital contributions
  • Member classifications
  • Admission of new members
3
Management Structure
  • Member-managed vs. manager-managed
  • Voting rights and procedures
  • Decision-making authority
  • Officer positions (if any)
  • Management meetings
4
Financial Provisions
  • Profit and loss distribution
  • Capital accounts
  • Additional contributions
  • Tax allocations
  • Financial reporting
5
Transfer Restrictions
  • Right of first refusal
  • Approval requirements
  • Valuation methods
  • Buyout procedures
  • Involuntary transfers
6
Dissolution & Exit
  • Dissolution triggers
  • Winding up procedures
  • Asset distribution
  • Member withdrawal
  • Death/disability provisions

Single-Member vs. Multi-Member LLCs

Operating agreements differ based on the number of members in your LLC.

Single-Member LLC
Simplified Structure:
  • One owner with full control
  • Simpler decision-making
  • Direct profit distribution
  • Easier management structure
Key Considerations:
  • Succession planning
  • Asset protection
  • Future member additions
  • Business continuity
Multi-Member LLC
Complex Structure:
  • Multiple owners with shared control
  • Voting and decision-making procedures
  • Profit/loss distribution rules
  • Member dispute resolution
Key Considerations:
  • Ownership percentages
  • Voting rights and procedures
  • Buyout and exit strategies
  • Conflict resolution methods
